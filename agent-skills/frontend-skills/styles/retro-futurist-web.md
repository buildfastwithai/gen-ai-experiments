# Retro Futurist Web

Design and implement interfaces that fuse retro digital nostalgia with current usability standards.

## Use When
- A user asks for retro-futuristic or synthwave-inspired UI.
- The product needs an experimental, identity-first web presence.
- A theme-driven launch page or creative portfolio is required.

## Inputs To Collect
- Era emphasis (80s synth, 90s terminal, early web).
- Intensity target (subtle tribute vs full thematic immersion).
- Core interactions and content goals.
- Accessibility thresholds for effects and motion.

## Workflow
1. Define era blend and modern usability guardrails.
2. Establish palette, type, and texture tokens (scanline/noise/glow).
3. Build modular sections with themed components.
4. Add constrained visual effects and responsive fallbacks.
5. Validate legibility, performance, and reduced-motion behavior.

## Quality Bar
- Effects must support brand identity, not dominate content.
- Maintain readable contrast and line lengths.
- Provide reduced-motion and low-effect fallbacks.
- Keep semantic HTML and keyboard support.

## Advanced Guidelines & Deep Dive
### Style Integration
- **Controlled Nostalgia**: Blend references rather than copy old interfaces literally.
- **Effect Budgeting**: Allocate effect intensity per section to avoid sensory fatigue.
- **Modern UX Backbone**: Keep navigation, CTAs, and forms intuitive and clear.

### Motion & Texture
- **Signal-Like Motion**: Use flicker or sweep effects sparingly for atmosphere.
- **Texture Layering**: Subtle grain and scanlines should sit behind content hierarchy.

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- **Unreadable Glow**: Neon effects that destroy text clarity.
- **Theme Over Utility**: Novelty must not block conversion paths.
- **Global Heavy Filters**: Full-page filters can hurt performance on low-end devices.
